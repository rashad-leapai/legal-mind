MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("NDA") is entered into on March 5, 2026, between TechCorp Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Company"), and DataVault Systems Inc., a Texas corporation ("Counterparty").

WHEREAS, the parties wish to explore a potential business relationship involving the exchange of confidential information;

NOW THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
1.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public, proprietary information disclosed by either party, including but not limited to:
- Technical data, algorithms, source code, and software architecture
- Business plans, financial information, and customer lists  
- Legal strategies, litigation plans, and settlement discussions
- Research and development projects and intellectual property

1.2 Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement
- Was rightfully known prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
- Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction

2. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY
2.1 Each party agrees to:
- Maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information received
- Use Confidential Information solely for evaluating potential business opportunities
- Limit access to employees and advisors with a legitimate need to know
- Apply the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but not less than reasonable care

2.2 Neither party shall reverse engineer, disassemble, or attempt to derive the composition or underlying information of any Confidential Information.

3. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES
3.1 Confidential Information may be disclosed if required by law, court order, or government regulation, provided that:
- The disclosing party provides prompt written notice to the other party
- The disclosing party cooperates in any effort to seek a protective order
- Disclosure is limited to the minimum required by the legal obligation

4. RETURN OF INFORMATION
4.1 Upon termination of discussions or upon written request, each party shall:
- Return or destroy all documents containing Confidential Information
- Delete all electronic copies and derivative works
- Provide written certification of compliance within thirty (30) days

5. NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED
5.1 This Agreement does not obligate either party to proceed with any transaction or business relationship.
5.2 No license or rights are granted except as expressly set forth herein.

6. REMEDIES
6.1 The parties acknowledge that disclosure of Confidential Information would cause irreparable harm.
6.2 Each party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief and monetary damages for any breach.
6.3 The prevailing party in any legal action shall be entitled to reasonable attorneys' fees.

7. TERM AND SURVIVAL
7.1 This Agreement shall remain in effect for three (3) years from the date of execution.
7.2 The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination and continue for five (5) years.
7.3 The obligations regarding return of information shall survive any termination.

8. GENERAL PROVISIONS
8.1 This Agreement shall be governed by Delaware law without regard to conflict of laws principles.
8.2 Any amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
8.3 If any provision is held unenforceable, the remainder shall remain in full force.
8.4 This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and electronically.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.
